FIGMA AI SITE DESIGN BRIEF — NCWQR REDESIGN

Project Overview
Design a full website for the National Center for Water Quality Research (NCWQR) at Heidelberg University. The site should be modern, educational, approachable, and interactive — modeled structurally after lakes.grace.edu but with NCWQR's own identity. The site serves three audiences: the general public, researchers/academics, and prospective students considering water quality research at Heidelberg University.

Color Palette
Primary colors drawn from NCWQR's brand: forest green (#2D5016 or similar), medium royal blue (#1B4F8A or similar), and earthy mud brown (#6B4C2A or similar). Use white and light gray as breathing room. Blue should dominate as the primary action/header color. Green for accents and nature-forward sections. Brown used sparingly for warmth in text or borders. The palette should feel cohesive — think field science meets academic credibility.

Typography
Clean, modern sans-serif for headings (e.g. Inter, DM Sans, or similar). Readable serif or sans-serif for body. Academic but approachable — not cold, not playful.

Site Structure & Pages
1. Homepage

Full-bleed hero section with a high-quality water/river/field photo background. Large headline: "Sustaining Our Soil and Water Resources". Subheadline: "A leader in surface and ground water research and monitoring in the Great Lakes region and beyond." Two CTA buttons: "Explore Our Research" and "Access the Data Portal."
Three-column section below hero: Monitoring | Research | Water Testing — each with an icon, one-sentence description, and a "Learn More" link. Modeled after lakes.grace.edu's Research/Education/Collaboration row.
"Why NCWQR Matters" section — short impactful stat block: 50+ years of data. 150,000+ water samples analyzed. 18 tributary monitoring stations. Informing NOAA's Lake Erie HAB forecasts. Display as bold numbers with labels, like a mission impact row.
"Latest News & Updates" — card-based news feed, 3 cards wide.
"Get Involved / Join Our Team" section — targeted at prospective students. Headline: "Do Real Science. Make a Real Impact." Short paragraph about student internship opportunities at NCWQR, link to Heidelberg University. Photo of students in the field if available.
Footer: Address (Gillmor Science Hall, Heidelberg University), contact info, social links (Facebook), link to Heidelberg University, nav links.

Design Patterns to Follow (modeled after lakes.grace.edu)

Section-based homepage with clear visual breaks between topics
Card grids for research projects and news
Dropdown navigation with subcategory links
Strong hero images on every page (not just homepage)
Icon + title + description pattern for service/program overviews
Stat/impact numbers displayed prominently
Clean footer with multiple nav column links
Mobile responsive layout throughout


Key Differentiator to Highlight Throughout
NCWQR has the longest-running, highest-frequency water quality monitoring dataset for Lake Erie tributaries in the world. That data powers NOAA's harmful algal bloom forecasts. This is the hook — it should appear on the homepage, the monitoring page, the about page, and anywhere a casual visitor might land. Make this feel significant, not buried.
